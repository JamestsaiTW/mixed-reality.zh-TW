# <a name="what-is-mixed-reality"></a><span data-ttu-id="9a803-104">什麼被混合實境？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-104">What is mixed reality?</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9a803-105">混合的實境是混合現實與數位世界的結果。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-105">Mixed reality is the result of blending the physical world with the digital world.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-106">混合的實境是下一代演進，人類看得、 電腦和環境的互動，並解除鎖定，之前只有我們 imaginations 的可能性。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-106">Mixed reality is the next evolution in human, computer, and environment interaction and unlocks possibilities that before now were restricted to our imaginations.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-107">它可大幅提升，在電腦視覺、 圖形化的處理能力、 顯示技術和輸入的系統。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-107">It is made possible by advancements in computer vision, graphical processing power, display technology, and input systems.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-108">詞彙*混合實境*由 Paul Milgram 和 Fumio Kishino 原本引入 1994年 （英文） 中"[分類的混合實境視覺顯示](http://etclab.mie.utoronto.ca/people/paul_dir/IEICE94/ieice.html)。 」</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-108">The term *mixed reality* was originally introduced in a 1994 paper by Paul Milgram and Fumio Kishino, "[A Taxonomy of Mixed Reality Visual Displays](http://etclab.mie.utoronto.ca/people/paul_dir/IEICE94/ieice.html)."</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-109">其紙張導入的概念*virtuality 連續體*，並著重於套用至的分類法分類的顯示方式。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-109">Their paper introduced the concept of the *virtuality continuum*, and focused on how the categorization of taxonomy applied to displays.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-110">從那時起，應用程式的混合實境超越顯示。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-110">Since then, the application of mixed reality goes beyond displays.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-111">它也包含環境的輸入、 空間音效和位置。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-111">It also includes environmental input, spatial sound, and location.</span></span>

## <a name="environmental-input-and-perception"></a><span data-ttu-id="9a803-112">環境的輸入和感覺</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-112">Environmental input and perception</span></span>

![Venn 圖表，顯示電腦、 人類和環境之間的互動](images/mixed-reality-venn-diagram-300px.png)<br> 

<span data-ttu-id="9a803-114">過去的幾個數十年來，人類與輸入的電腦之間的關聯性已也探索了。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-114">Over the past several decades, the relationship between human and computer input has been well explored.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-115">甚至還有稱為廣泛研究專業領域*人工電腦互動*或 HCI。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-115">It even has a widely studied discipline known as *human computer interaction* or HCI.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-116">輸入會透過各種方式，包括鍵盤、 滑鼠、 touch、 筆墨、 語音及甚至 Kinect 骨架追蹤。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-116">Human input happens through a variety of means, including keyboards, mice, touch, ink, voice, and even Kinect skeletal tracking.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9a803-117">在感應器與處理方面的改進，讓上升的電腦輸入新的區域從環境。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-117">Advancements in sensors and processing are giving rise to a new area of computer input from environments.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-118">電腦和環境之間的互動是有效的環境了解或*perception*。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-118">The interaction between computers and environments is effectively environmental understanding or *perception*.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-119">因此稱為顯示環境的資訊的 Windows 中的 API 名稱[perception Api](https://docs.microsoft.com/uwp/api/Windows.Perception)。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-119">Hence the API names in Windows that reveal environmental information are called the [perception APIs](https://docs.microsoft.com/uwp/api/Windows.Perception).</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-120">環境的輸入擷取之類的世界中的個人位置 (例如[前端追蹤](coordinate-systems.md))，介面和界限 (例如[空間對應](spatial-mapping.md)和[空間了解](case-study-expanding-the-spatial-mapping-capabilities-of-hololens.md))，環境光源、 環境的音效、 物件辨識和位置。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-120">Environmental input captures things like a person's position in the world (e.g. [head tracking](coordinate-systems.md)), surfaces and boundaries (e.g. [spatial mapping](spatial-mapping.md) and [spatial understanding](case-study-expanding-the-spatial-mapping-capabilities-of-hololens.md)), ambient lighting, environmental sound, object recognition, and location.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9a803-121">現在，所有三個-電腦處理的組合，人性化的輸入和環境的輸入-設定建立，則為 true 的混合的實境體驗的機會。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-121">Now, the combination of all three--computer processing, human input, and environmental input--sets the opportunity to create true mixed reality experiences.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-122">透過真實世界的移動可以轉譯為數位世界中移動。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-122">Movement through the physical world can translate to movement in the digital world.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-123">在真實世界的界限可能會影響應用程式體驗，例如遊戲，數位世界中。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-123">Boundaries in the physical world can influence application experiences, such as game play, in the digital world.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-124">不需要環境的輸入經驗無法 blend 之間實體與數位現況。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-124">Without environmental input, experiences cannot blend between physical and digital realities.</span></span>

## <a name="the-mixed-reality-spectrum"></a><span data-ttu-id="9a803-125">混合的實境範圍</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-125">The mixed reality spectrum</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9a803-126">混合的實境兼具實體與數位世界，因為這些兩個的現實情況會定義稱為 virtuality 連續體的範圍極座標圖的結尾。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-126">Since mixed reality blends both physical and digital worlds, these two realities define the polar ends of a spectrum known as the virtuality continuum.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-127">為了簡單起見，我們將它作為*混合的實境頻譜*。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-127">For simplicity, we refer to this as the *mixed reality spectrum*.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-128">在左手邊有實體實際上以人類的我們存在;在右手邊，我們會有相對應的數位現實。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-128">On the left-hand side we have physical reality in which we, humans, exist; on the right-hand side we have the corresponding digital reality.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9a803-129">現今市場上大部分的行動電話都有一些不到環境了解功能。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-129">Most mobile phones on the market today have little to no environmental understanding capabilities.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-130">因此，它們提供的體驗不能混用實體與數位現況之間。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-130">Thus the experiences they offer cannot mix between physical and digital realities.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-131">重疊的真實世界的視訊資料流上的圖形體驗*擴增實境*。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-131">The experiences that overlay graphics on video streams of the physical world are *augmented reality*.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-132">Occlude 呈現的數位體驗您的檢視體驗*虛擬實境*。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-132">The experiences that occlude your view to present a digital experience are *virtual reality*.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-133">如您所見，這兩項極端之間啟用體驗已*混合實境*:</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-133">As you can see, the experiences enabled between these two extremes is *mixed reality*:</span></span>
* <span data-ttu-id="9a803-134">從開始真實世界中，放置在數位的物件，例如雷射，如同它是真的有。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-134">Starting with the physical world, placing a digital object, such as a hologram, as if it was really there.</span></span>
* <span data-ttu-id="9a803-135">開頭為真實世界中，另一個人-數位表示顯示圖片，顯示它們已在其中釐離開資訊時的位置。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-135">Starting with the physical world, a digital representation of another person--an avatar--shows the location where they were standing when leaving notes.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-136">換句話說，體驗表示非同步的共同作業在不同時間點的時間。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-136">In other words, experiences that represent asynchronous collaboration at different points in time.</span></span>
* <span data-ttu-id="9a803-137">從開始數位世界，實體界限從真實世界中，例如牆壁和 furniture，就會出現數位內體驗，可協助使用者避免實體的物件。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-137">Starting with a digital world, physical boundaries from the physical world, such as walls and furniture, appear digitally within the experience to help users avoid physical objects.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9a803-139">最擴增的實境和虛擬實境供應項目可立即代表這個範圍非常小的一部分。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-139">Most augmented reality and virtual reality offerings available today represent a very small part of this spectrum.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-140">是，不過，較大的混合的實境範圍子集。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-140">They are, however, subsets of the larger mixed reality spectrum.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-141">Windows 10 使用納入考量，廣泛所建置，並允許透明混色的人員、 地點及項目與真實世界的數位表示法。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-141">Windows 10 is built with the entire spectrum in mind, and allows blending digital representations of people, places, and things with the real world.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9a803-143">有兩種類型的裝置可提供 Windows Mixed Reality 體驗：</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-143">There are two main types of devices that deliver Windows Mixed Reality experiences:</span></span>

## <a name="devices-and-experiences"></a><span data-ttu-id="9a803-164">裝置和體驗</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-164">Devices and experiences</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9a803-165">技術演進是已啟用混合的實境體驗的內容。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-165">Technological advancement is what has enabled mixed reality experiences.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-166">沒有任何裝置現在可以執行跨整個範圍的體驗。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-166">There are no devices today that can run experiences across the entire spectrum.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-167">不過，Windows 10 提供通用的混合的實境平台為裝置製造商和開發人員。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-167">However, Windows 10 provides a common mixed reality platform for both device manufacturers and developers.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-168">裝置目前可支援混合的實境範圍內的特定範圍。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-168">Devices today can support a specific range within the mixed reality spectrum.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-169">經過一段時間，新的裝置將會展開該範圍內。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-169">Over time, new devices will expand that range.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-170">在未來，全像攝影版的裝置會變成更沈浸式，而且沈浸式裝置會變成更全像攝影版。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-170">In the future, holographic devices will become more immersive, and immersive devices will become more holographic.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="9a803-172">通常，最好考慮何種體驗的應用程式或遊戲的開發人員想要建立。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-172">Often, it is best to think what type of experience an application or game developer wants to create.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-173">特定點或範圍上的組件，通常會以目標的體驗。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-173">The experiences will typically target a specific point or part on the spectrum.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-174">然後，開發人員應該考慮他們想要為目標的裝置的功能。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-174">Then, developers should consider the capabilities of devices they want to target.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="9a803-175">比方說，真實世界所依賴的體驗將會執行最佳 HoloLens 上。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="9a803-175">For example, experiences that rely on the physical world will run best on HoloLens.</span></span>
